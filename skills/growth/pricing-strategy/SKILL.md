## Purpose

Develop a pricing strategy grounded in **value-based principles** — understanding willingness-to-pay, choosing the right pricing model, setting price points, and designing packaging that captures value while growing the customer base.

---

## Application

### Pricing Strategy Template

```markdown
# Pricing Strategy: [Product Name]

## 1. Value Assessment
### Value to Customer
| Customer Segment | Primary Value Delivered | Quantified Value ($) |
|-----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| [Segment 1] | [Value] | [$X saved/earned per month] |
| [Segment 2] | [Value] | [$X saved/earned per month] |

### Willingness-to-Pay Research
**Method used:** [Van Westendorp / Conjoint / Competitor benchmarking / Customer interviews]
| Price Point | Too cheap | Cheap | Expensive | Too expensive |
|------------|-----------|-------|-----------|--------------|
| [Segment 1] | $[X] | $[X] | $[X] | $[X] |

## 2. Pricing Model
**Chosen model:** [Per seat / Usage / Flat / Tiered / Freemium / Hybrid]
**Value metric:** [What unit aligns price with value received]
**Why this model:** [Rationale tied to value delivery pattern]

## 3. Price Architecture
| Tier | Price | Target Segment | Value Metric Limit | Key Features |
|------|-------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|

**Price positioning strategy:** [Premium / Market rate / Penetration / Freemium-led]

## 5. Revenue Impact Model
| Scenario | Users | Conv Rate | ARPU | MRR |
|----------|-------|----------|------|-----|
```

---

## Quality Rubric

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Value-Based** | Cost-plus or gut feel | Competitor benchmarking | Willingness-to-pay research |
| **Packaging** | One-size-fits-all | Basic tiers | Segment-aligned tiers with value metric |
| **Validation** | Untested | Competitive comparison | Research + experiments planned |
| **Revenue Model** | No projections | Single scenario | 3 scenarios with assumptions |
