## Purpose

Build influence as a PM without formal authority — the **defining skill** of product management — using trust, evidence, alignment, and strategic communication to get cross-functional teams to follow your lead.

---

## Application

### The Influence Stack

```markdown
## The Influence Stack (bottom-up, build in order)

### 1. Trust (Foundation)
- [ ] Deliver on commitments consistently
- [ ] Be transparent about unknowns and risks
- [ ] Give credit generously, take blame personally
- [ ] Show genuine interest in others' expertise

### 2. Evidence (Credibility)
- [ ] Lead with data, not opinions
- [ ] Share user research and customer quotes
- [ ] Show ROI/impact projections
- [ ] Acknowledge when you're wrong

### 3. Alignment (Shared Goals)
- [ ] Connect your ask to their goals/OKRs
- [ ] Frame decisions as "us" not "me vs. you"
- [ ] Find the win-win before the meeting
- [ ] Understand their constraints

### 4. Communication (Execution)
- [ ] Tailor your message to the audience
- [ ] Use their language (technical for eng, business for execs)
- [ ] Be concise — respect their time
- [ ] Make the decision easy (provide a recommendation)
```

### Common Scenarios

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Eng says "no"** | Understand the constraint; negotiate scope, not deadline |
| **Exec overrides** | Ask "what would convince you?"; bring data next time |
| **Sales wants feature X** | "Let's test the hypothesis behind that request" |
| **Design disagrees** | "Let's prototype both and user-test" |
| **Team is burned out** | Protect them; cut scope; show you care about sustainability |

---

## Quality Rubric

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Trust** | Unreliable | Sometimes delivers | Consistently trustworthy |
| **Evidence** | "I think" | Some data | Data-driven with user research |
| **Alignment** | "Do what I say" | Explains rationale | Connects to their goals |
| **Communication** | Same pitch for everyone | Audience-aware | Tailored + concise per audience |
