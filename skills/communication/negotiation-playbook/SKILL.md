## Purpose

Navigate product decisions through **stakeholder negotiation** — using interest-based bargaining, BATNA analysis, and conflict resolution to get buy-in without burning political capital.

---

## Application

### Pre-Negotiation Prep

```markdown
# Negotiation Prep: [Decision/Topic]

## What I Want
- **Position:** [My stated request]
- **Interest:** [My underlying need — why I want this]

## What They Want
- **Their stated position:** [What they're requesting]
- **Their underlying interest:** [What they actually care about]

## BATNA (Best Alternative to No Agreement)
- **My BATNA:** [What happens if we can't agree]
- **Their BATNA:** [What happens for them if no agreement]

## ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
- [Where our interests overlap — the win-win zone]

## Strategy
- **Opening:** [How to frame the conversation]
- **Concessions I can make:** [Things I can give that cost me little]
- **Non-negotiables:** [What I won't compromise on and why]
- **Fallback position:** [My acceptable minimum outcome]
```

### Common PM Negotiation Scenarios

| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| **Exec wants feature X on the roadmap** | Reframe around outcomes; offer to test the hypothesis |
| **Eng says it takes too long** | Negotiate scope (MVP), not timeline |
| **Sales promises features to close deals** | Create a feedback loop; propose lightweight validation |
| **Design disagrees on approach** | Propose user test to let data decide |
| **Two stakeholders want opposite things** | Find the underlying interest; propose A/B test |
